exam in 2 hours and i just realised i have no idea how to do integration by substitution send help 😰
💾 Remembered
You nailed differentiation last week — substitution is just running that backwards. You've got this.

Two hours is enough. Here's the method in 3 steps — no fluff:

1. Spot a chunk inside the integral that looks like it could be u
2. Write u = that chunk, then find du/dx and rearrange for dx
3. Substitute everything in, integrate the clean version, put u back

Want a worked example right now?
yes please — like ∫2x(x²+1)⁵ dx
Let u = x² + 1, so du/dx = 2x, meaning dx = du/2x

Substitute: ∫2x · u⁵ · du/2x = ∫u⁵ du

Integrate: u⁶/6 + c

Put u back: (x²+1)⁶/6 + c

The 2x cancels — that's the trick the examiners love. See it?
omg yes that makes sense now
